The Crew

Behind every expedition is a team of passionate adventurers dedicated to ensuring a positive end goal. Meet the stellar team who will be hitting the road and trail to tackle the 14,000 mile Tour De Fox in support of The Michael J. Fox Foundation:

 

Sam Fox - Athlete

Over three months Sam will bike more than 5,000 miles and climb 49 different mountain peaks. This means Sam will ride an average of 120 miles and climb more than 1 peak every two days on the road. Sam has been planning this adventure for months, but come May 20, his focus will shift purely to biking, climbing, and meeting as many dedicated members of the PD community and volunteers as possible.

Prior to joining The Michael J. Fox Foundation staff in 2012, Sam was an avid Team Fox member dedicated to transforming his active lifestyle into a means to raise funds and awareness in support of his mother, Lucy, who lives with Parkinson’s. Sam has completed two extreme challenges for Team Fox since 2011: a 60-day, 2,500-mile adventure run from Canada to Mexico on the Pacific Crest Trail, and a single day, 100-mile run from New York City to the tip of Long Island. The Tour De Fox promises to be his most challenging undertaking to date.

Matt Mitchell - Athlete

Matt will join the Tour crew in mid-July in Jackson, Wyoming. He'll join Sam on every climb and every bike ride for the final month of the journey, and he'll do all of this as a volunteer. Consider this a circuitous homecoming for Matt, who has been living in Finland for two years, but is a native of Vancouver, BC, the last stop on the Tour De Fox.

Matt has been a Team Fox member for 4 years. He is an accomplished endurance athlete and has run marathons for Team Fox all over the world. He got involved with Team Fox in 2010 to honor his father, who passed with Parkinson's in the same year. Matt, a familiar face to most in the Team Fox community, has come to define volunteer dedication to The Michael j. Fox Foundation. The Tour crew is lucky to have him on board.

Anne Lovelace - Support Director

Anne is the Support Director for the Tour De Fox. This means she is the boss on the road. She'll be tasked with ensuring that all members of the Tour Crew make it from point A to Point B (whether that be from city to city, or from valley floor to mountaintop) on time, and in one piece.

Anne is an experienced climber and expedition leader. She is a certified EMT with a specialty in Wilderness First Response. Anne Spent the last year as a healthcare consultant in rural Kenya and will start Medical School right after the Tour De Fox wraps up in August. She has no direct connection to Parkinson's disease but is excited to work toward the mission of the Michael J Fox Foundation.

Ryan Holmes - Support Crew

Prior to joining The Michael J. Fox Foundation staff in 2012, Sam was an avid Team Fox member dedicated to transforming his active lifestyle into a means to raise funds and awareness in support of his mother, Lucy, who lives with Parkinson’s. In 2011, Sam’s first extreme adventure encompassed a 60-day run from Canada to Mexico, covering all 2,500 miles of the Pacific Crest Trail. In 2012, Sam was back at it with his ‘Hamptons Hundred Miler’: a one day, 100-mile run from New York City to the tip of Long Island.

So why the extreme adventures? “Physical challenges are a way for me to feel closer to my Mom and, in a way, to understand her experience with PD. It’s cliché, but when I’m tired and done, I just think about what my Mom goes through every day and I find the motivation to take that next step.”
