Climb Humphrey's Peak - 12,637 ft.

August 14, 2015

Humphrey's Peak is the highest point in Arizona. Most people picture a lone cactus in a desert expanse when they think of Arizona and don't realize that the northern part of the state has pine forests, high mountain peaks, snowy winters, and relatively mild summers. Humphrey's Peak is located just outside of Flagstaff, AZ. Its summit is easily accessible thanks to the trails of a ski resort on its northern slopes. Looking north from the top the Grand Canyon is plainly, and spectacularly visible.

When: August 14, 2015. 7 AM

Where: Humphrey's peak Trailhead. Arizona Snowbowl, north of Flagstaff, AZ. View map

The route: 7 mile round-trip hike on the Humphrey's Peak Trail. the trail is well maintained, and runs parallel to the ski slopes of the Arizona Snowbowl resort.

climb with sam

No hiking or climbing experience is necessary. Climbers should be in good physical condition and should be prepared for unprdictable weather and muddy or snowy trail conditions.
